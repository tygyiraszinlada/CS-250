# CS 250 Reflection

## How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by communicating with stakeholders and understanding what they expect the software to accomplish. User stories help translate those needs into clear and manageable requirements from the user's perspective. They also make it easier to prioritize features and ensure development focuses on delivering value to customers.

## How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach development by breaking large tasks into smaller goals and focusing on continuous improvement. Agile practices such as sprint planning, daily standups and retrospectives help keep projects organized and adaptable. In future projects, I plan to continue using user stories, iterative development and regular feedback to improve both efficiency and software quality.

## What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating openly, supporting others and taking responsibility for assigned work. Strong collaboration, respect and accountability help teams overcome challenges and achieve shared goals. I believe successful software development depends on trust, willingness to learn and helping the team succeed as a whole.
